## Overview

Track your arms and armor collection with detailed fields for item type, era, maker, material, and condition. Perfect for collectors of historical weapons, military antiques, edged weapons, and armor.

<Card title="Arms & Armor Features" icon="shield">

  - Era and period dating
  - Maker/manufacturer identification
  - Material and construction
  - Condition assessment
  - Provenance and history
  - Market value tracking
</Card>

## Arms & Armor Fields

### Identification

<ParamField path="item_type" type="string">
  Type or classification of the item

  **Edged Weapons:**
  - Sword (longsword, rapier, saber, katana)
  - Dagger
  - Knife
  - Bayonet
  - Polearm (halberd, pike, lance)

  **Firearms:**
  - Pistol
  - Rifle
  - Musket
  - Revolver
  - Shotgun

  **Armor:**
  - Helmet
  - Breastplate
  - Gauntlets
  - Full suit of armor
  - Shield
  - Chainmail

  **Other:**
  - Military equipment
  - Accessories
  - Insignia
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="era" type="string">
  Time period or era of the item

  **Examples:**
  - Medieval (500-1500 CE)
  - Renaissance (1400-1600)
  - 17th Century
  - American Civil War (1861-1865)
  - World War I (1914-1918)
  - World War II (1939-1945)
  - Edo Period Japan (1603-1868)
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="maker" type="string">
  Maker, manufacturer, or armorer

  **Examples:**
  - Wilkinson Sword
  - Samuel Colt
  - Springfield Armory
  - Unknown German maker
  - Attributed to Augsburg workshops
  - Imperial Japanese Arsenal
</ParamField>

### Physical Details

<ParamField path="material" type="string">
  Primary materials of construction

  **Examples:**
  - Steel blade with brass guard
  - Blued steel and walnut
  - Damascus steel
  - Iron with leather fittings
  - Bronze and steel
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="condition" type="select">
  Overall condition of the item

  **Grading:**
  - **Excellent** - Exceptional, minimal wear
  - **Very Good** - Light wear, well preserved
  - **Good** - Some wear, structurally sound
  - **Fair** - Notable wear or damage
  - **Poor** - Significant damage or deterioration

  **Condition notes should include:**
  - Blade/barrel condition
  - Surface patina
  - Any repairs or restorations
  - Functionality (if applicable)
  - Missing parts
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="provenance" type="textarea">
  History and previous ownership

  **Include:**
  - Military unit or officer (if known)
  - Previous collectors
  - Auction history
  - Documentation or certificates
  - Historical significance
</ParamField>

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Document Thoroughly" icon="file-text">
    Create complete records:
    - Measurements and specifications
    - Maker's marks and stamps
    - Serial numbers (if applicable)
    - Historical attributions
    - Previous owners and provenance
    - Expert opinions or certificates

    <Tip>
      Detailed documentation helps with authentication and insurance
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Photograph Completely" icon="camera">
    Capture detailed photos:
    - Overall views (multiple angles)
    - Maker's marks and signatures
    - Blade/barrel details
    - Condition issues
    - Scale reference
    - Any accessories

    <Note>
      Good photography is essential for insurance and potential sales
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Store Properly" icon="shield">
    Protect your collection:
    - Climate-controlled storage
    - Avoid humidity extremes
    - Proper supports and stands
    - Light oiling for steel (careful with antiques)
    - Secure storage for firearms

    <Warning>
      Never clean antiques without expert advice - original patina is valuable
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Legal Compliance" icon="gavel">
    Be aware of regulations:
    - Antique firearm laws (vary by jurisdiction)
    - Import/export restrictions
    - Registration requirements
    - Transportation rules
    - Display regulations

    <Note>
      Consult local laws for firearms and edged weapons in your area
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Seek Expert Authentication" icon="certificate">
    For valuable pieces:
    - Consult specialists
    - Obtain written opinions
    - Check for known fakes in the category
    - Verify provenance claims
    - Consider professional appraisal
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Common Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I value arms & armor?" icon="dollar-sign">
    **Valuation resources:**
    - Specialist auction results (Bonhams, Rock Island)
    - Dealer pricing
    - Price guides and references
    - Professional appraisals
    - Collector community knowledge

    **Factors affecting value:**
    - Rarity and historical significance
    - Maker attribution
    - Condition
    - Provenance
    - Completeness (original parts)
    - Documentation
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Should I restore or clean antiques?" icon="spray-can">
    **Generally, no:**
    - Original patina is valuable
    - Amateur restoration destroys value
    - Cleaning can be irreversible
    - Consult conservators for any work

    **Safe maintenance:**
    - Light dusting only
    - Careful handling
    - Climate control
    - Professional conservation when needed
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I authenticate Japanese swords?" icon="certificate">
    **Authentication resources:**
    - NBTHK (Japanese certification)
    - NTHK (alternative certification)
    - Specialist dealers
    - Published references
    - Expert opinions

    **Papers to look for:**
    - Hozon (worthy of preservation)
    - Tokubetsu Hozon (especially worthy)
    - Juyo (important)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
