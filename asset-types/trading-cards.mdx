## Overview

Track your trading card collection with detailed fields for sport/game, player, card set, grade, and grading service. Perfect for sports card collectors, TCG enthusiasts, and investors managing graded cards and vintage collections.

<Card title="Trading Card Features" icon="cards">
  - Sport/game classification
  - Player or character tracking
  - Card set and year
  - Grade and grading service (PSA, BGS, CGC)
  - Population reports
  - Market value tracking
</Card>

## Adding a Trading Card

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Add Item">
    Click "Add Item" from your dashboard or go to [storehouses.app/add](https://storehouses.app/add)

  <Step title="Select Trading Cards">
    Choose "Trading Cards" as the category

  <Step title="Enter Details">

### Identification

<ParamField path="sport_game" type="string">

  - Non-sport (entertainment)
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="player_character" type="string">
  Player name or character featured

  **Examples:**
  - Michael Jordan
  - Mickey Mantle
  - Charizard
  - Black Lotus
  - LeBron James
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="card_set" type="string">
  Card set or product name

  **Examples:**
  - 1952 Topps
  - 1986-87 Fleer
  - Base Set Unlimited
  - Prizm Silver
  - National Treasures
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="year" type="number">
  Year the card was produced

  **Examples:**
  - 1952 (Topps Mickey Mantle)
  - 1986 (Fleer Michael Jordan RC)
  - 1999 (Base Set Charizard)
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="card_number" type="string">
  Card number within the set

  **Examples:**
  - #311 (1952 Topps Mantle)
  - #57 (1986 Fleer Jordan)
  - #4/102 (Base Set Charizard)
</ParamField>

### Grading

<ParamField path="grade" type="string">
  Numeric grade

  **PSA Scale (1-10):**
  - **PSA 10** - Gem Mint
  - **PSA 9** - Mint
  - **PSA 8** - Near Mint-Mint
  - **PSA 7** - Near Mint
  - **PSA 6** - Excellent-Mint
  - **PSA 5** - Excellent
  - **PSA 4** - Very Good-Excellent
  - **PSA 3** - Very Good
  - **PSA 2** - Good
  - **PSA 1** - Poor

  **BGS uses subgrades:** Centering, Corners, Edges, Surface
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="grading_service" type="string">
  Third-party grading service

  **Major Services:**
  - **PSA** - Professional Sports Authenticator (most popular)
  - **BGS** - Beckett Grading Services
  - **SGC** - Sportscard Guaranty Corporation
  - **CGC** - Certified Guaranty Company (TCG focus)

  <Tip>
    PSA 10s and BGS 10s (Black Label) command the highest premiums
  </Tip>
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="certification_number" type="string">
  Grading service certification number

  **Use:** Allows verification and population lookup
</ParamField>

### Special Designations

<ParamField path="parallel" type="string">
  Parallel or variation type

  **Examples:**
  - Base
  - Silver Prizm
  - Gold /10
  - 1st Edition
  - Holo
  - Refractor
  - Shadowless
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="auto_patch" type="string">
  Autograph and patch details

  **Examples:**
  - On-card Auto
  - Patch Auto /25
  - Rookie Patch Auto (RPA)
  - Sticker Auto
</ParamField>

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Get Cards Graded" icon="certificate">
    For valuable cards ($50+ raw):
    - PSA is most popular for sports
    - BGS for premium look and subgrades
    - CGC gaining for Pokemon/TCG
    - Grading protects and authenticates

    <Warning>
      Grading wait times can be long - factor this into your planning
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Store Properly" icon="shield">
    Protect your collection:
    - Penny sleeves + top loaders for raw cards
    - Keep graded cards in cases/boxes
    - Control temperature and humidity
    - Avoid direct sunlight
    - Never bend or touch card surface
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Research Values" icon="search">
    Stay informed on market:
    - Check eBay sold listings
    - PSA/BGS pop reports
    - 130point.com for sales data
    - Card Ladder for price trends
    - Market Movers for Pokemon
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Document Everything" icon="file-text">
    Record for each card:
    - Full card description
    - Grade and cert number
    - Purchase price and date
    - Front/back photos
    - Any special variations
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Common Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="PSA vs BGS - which is better?" icon="scale-balanced">
    **PSA:**
    - Most recognized and liquid
    - Simple 1-10 scale
    - Higher premiums for PSA 10s
    - Best for most sports cards

    **BGS:**
    - Subgrades add detail
    - Black Label 10s very prestigious
    - Popular for modern high-end
    - Tuxedo cases look premium

    **Choose based on:** What you collect and target market
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is this card worth grading?" icon="certificate">
    **Consider grading if:**
    - Raw value is $50+
    - Card appears high grade (8+)
    - You plan to sell eventually
    - It's a key rookie or chase card

    **Skip grading if:**
    - Low value or common card
    - Obvious condition issues
    - Grading cost exceeds value increase
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I spot fakes?" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    **Red flags:**
    - Price too good to be true
    - Seller with no history
    - Wrong card stock or finish
    - Font or printing issues
    - No certification verification

    **Protection:**
    - Buy graded when possible
    - Verify cert numbers online
    - Purchase from reputable sellers
    - Know the authentic characteristics
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
