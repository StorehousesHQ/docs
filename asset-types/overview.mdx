## Choosing the Right Category

Select the category that best matches your item. While some items could fit multiple categories, choose based on the primary characteristic:

### Decision Guide

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Precious Metals vs. Jewelry" icon="coins">
    **Choose Precious Metals if**:
    - Primarily valued for metal weight and purity
    - Investment bullion (coins, bars, rounds)
    - Primarily tracking spot value

    **Choose Fine Jewelry if**:
    - Designer or branded jewelry
    - Valued for craftsmanship and design
    - Contains multiple materials or gemstones
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Gems vs. Jewelry" icon="gem">
    **Choose Gemstones (Gems) if**:
    - Loose, unmounted gemstones
    - Tracking carat weight and certification

    **Choose Fine Jewelry if**:
    - Gem is mounted in jewelry
    - Tracking the complete piece
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Coins vs. Precious Metals" icon="money-bill">
    **Choose Coins & Currency if**:
    - Numismatic value exceeds metal content
    - Tracking grade and grading service
    - Collecting for rarity, not weight

    **Choose Metals & Bullion if**:
    - Primarily valued for metal content
    - Modern bullion coins (Eagles, Maples, etc.)
    - Tracking weight and spot price
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Manuscripts vs. Fine Art" icon="book">
    **Choose Rare Books & Manuscripts if**:
    - Books, letters, or documents
    - Tracking author and publication details
    - Binding type and edition matter

    **Choose Fine Art if**:
    - Paintings, sculptures, or prints
    - Artist and medium are primary identifiers
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Artifacts vs. Arms & Armor" icon="landmark">
    **Choose Artifacts if**:
    - Archaeological or cultural objects
    - Tracking origin culture and period
    - General historical items

    **Choose Arms & Armor if**:
    - Specifically weapons or armor
    - Military or martial items
    - Tracking maker and era
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Other Collectibles" icon="box">
    Use "Other Collectibles" for items that don't fit the specialized categories:
    - Musical instruments
    - Memorabilia
    - Antique furniture
    - Sports equipment
    - Toys and games
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Category-Specific Features

### Automatic Valuation

<Tip>
  **Precious Metals** get automatic valuation based on current spot prices, weight, and purity. Values update daily as market prices change.
</Tip>

### Certification Tracking

**Gemstones** includes fields for:
- Certification body (GIA, AGS, etc.)
- Certificate number
- Grading details (cut, color, clarity, carat)

### Provenance Documentation

**Fine Art** and **Arms & Armor** include provenance fields to document:
- Previous owners
- Exhibition history
- Authentication documentation
- Historical significance

### Vintage & Age Tracking

**Wine** and **Spirits** track:
- Vintage year
- Producer/distillery
- Age statements
- Storage conditions
- Optimal drinking windows

## Switching Categories

If you need to change an item's category:

<Warning>
  Changing an item's category will reset category-specific fields. Common fields (title, purchase price, etc.) are preserved.
</Warning>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Item">
    Navigate to the item's detail page
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Edit">
    Click the edit button
  </Step>

  <Step title="Change Category">
    Select a new category from the dropdown
  </Step>

  <Step title="Re-enter Specific Data">
    Fill in the new category's specific fields
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    Save the updated item
  </Step>
</Steps>
